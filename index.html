Why this route

Porto→Santiago is ~271km — at 4-5 walking hours/day, only about half fits on foot. So each day targets one standout stretch — boardwalks, the Apúlia stilt granaries, the Rio Minho crossing into Spain, Cabo Silleiro lighthouse, the Pontevedra ría woodlands — and taxis cover the connective bits that are just more of the same scenery or plain road walking. No day exceeds the 4-5h cap.

Day by day

  • Sun 27 Sep Arrivaltravel
    Chennai uncles land Lisbon 7:25am — booked, Etihad EY347 MAA→AUH (21:55 Sep 26 → 00:35), EY99 AUH→LIS (02:10 → 07:25) — immigration/bags by 8:15, check into a Lisbon hotel (near Santa Apolónia station, for the early train next morning), drop bags. Leave ~12:15 for Sintra (~35 min), lunch at Arun's home 13:00-15:00, afternoon in Sintra. Balamurugan lands separately, 2:50pm (TAP nonstop, SFO, tentative), joins in Sintra or Cascais by ~17:30. Cascais ~17:00 for the marina, dinner 19:30-21:30, back to the Lisbon hotel ~22:15.
  • Mon 28 Sep Póvoa de Varzimwalk · 14km · ~3.5h
    IC train Lisboa Santa Apolónia → Porto Campanhã, ~3h — 07:00 (arr. ~10:00) or 08:00 (arr. ~11:10) both work well; confirm exact times closer to the date. Then taxi Porto → Labruge and walk in.
    Taxi skips ~16km of Porto's industrial port/Matosinhos stretch — pure city walking, nothing scenic. The walk is the start of the coastal boardwalks (passadiços) — flat wooden walkways along the dunes, one of the most photogenic, easiest stretches on the Camino.
  • Tue 29 Sep Viana do Castelowalk · 16km · ~4h
    Taxi to Aguçadoura, walk to Marinhas via Apúlia, taxi on to Viana do Castelo.
    Passes Apúlia's palheiros — centuries-old stilt granaries over the beach, the most photographed spot on this coast. Taxi trims a plain residential stretch before, then the last ~13km into Viana (pleasant, but repeat scenery).
  • Wed 30 Sep A Guarda / Oia, Spainwalk · ~18km · ~4.5h
    Taxi Viana → Caminha, walk in from there.
    Taxi skips the 26.8km Viana–Caminha stage — nice but repeat scenery. The walk starts with a ferry across the Rio Minho into Spain, then wild cliff-top coastline past A Guarda to Oia's monastery at the water's edge — the most scenic stage on the route.
  • Thu 1 Oct Vigowalk · 16km · ~4h
    Walk on to Baiona via the Cabo Silleiro lighthouse, taxi Baiona → Vigo.
    Lighthouse headland walk into Baiona, one of Galicia's prettiest fishing towns. Taxi covers the ~21km into Vigo — a working port city, worth arriving in, not walking into.
  • Fri 2 Oct Redondelawalk · 15.4km · ~4h
    Walk straight from Vigo — no taxi needed today.
    Quiet coastal and forest paths — also where the traditional "last 100km" pilgrim stretch begins.
  • Sat 3 Oct Pontevedrawalk · 19.8km · ~5h
    Walk straight through — no taxi needed today.
    One of the most beautiful stages on the Camino — woodland paths along the Ría de Pontevedra.
  • Sun 4 Oct Caldas de Reiswalk · ~17km · ~4.5h
    Taxi a few km out of Pontevedra, then walk in.
    Taxi skips a short, unremarkable exit from Pontevedra. Caldas de Reis is a classic pilgrim village with thermal springs.
  • Mon 5 Oct Padrónwalk · 18.6km · ~4.5h
    Walk straight from Caldas de Reis — no taxi needed today.
    A historic town tied to the Camino's founding legend — where St. James's body is said to have arrived by boat.
  • Tue 6 Oct Santiago — finishwalk · ~16km · ~4h
    Taxi partway out of Padrón, walk the rest of the way in.
    Taxi skips ~8km of flat, unremarkable stretch out of Padrón. The walk in ends at the Cathedral — Pilgrim Mass, celebration dinner.

Expected weather

Late Sep in Portugal is warm — Porto highs ~22-24°C, lows ~14-15°C, mostly dry. Into Galicia and October it cools and wets up: Santiago highs ~19-20°C, lows ~10-11°C, rain on roughly half of October days historically. Pack for dry warmth early and rain/wind later.

What to pack

Feet

Broken-in walking shoes or trail runners (not brand new). 2-3 pairs moisture-wicking socks. Blister kit (moleskin/tape) — the most-skipped item people regret.

Clothing

Quick-dry t-shirts/base layers, one light fleece or insulating layer for cool mornings, comfortable trousers/shorts, one decent outfit for dinners.

Rain & wind

Packable rain jacket (essential, especially Oct 2 onward), a cap or buff for coastal wind, a small dry bag or pack cover for phone/electronics.

Daily kit

Small daypack (20-25L is plenty — bags transfer separately, see Luggage above), refillable water bottle, sunscreen & sunglasses, phone charger + EU plug adapter (Type C/F, 230V), some cash for rural stops.
