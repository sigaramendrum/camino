Why this route

The full Porto→Santiago Camino is around 290km — at a comfortable 4-5 hours of walking a day, there's only time to cover roughly half of it on foot. So this route is deliberately selective: every walking day targets the single most scenic, most distinctive stretch in that area — boardwalks, a centuries-old stilt-granary beach, a river crossing into a new country, a cliff-top lighthouse, the ría woodlands into Pontevedra — and a taxi covers the connective bits in between that would just be more of the same scenery, or plain road walking, without adding anything new. Every day stays within the 4-5 hour cap; nobody's grinding out a 6-hour slog to tick off a stage that isn't worth it.

Day by day

  • Sun 27 Sep Arrivaltravel
    Arrive Lisbon. Lunch at Arun's home in Sintra, afternoon in Sintra/Cascais, evening transfer to Porto.
  • Mon 28 Sep Póvoa de Varzimwalk · 14km · ~3.5h
    Taxi Porto → Labruge, then walk in.
    Taxi skips ~16km of Porto's industrial port/Matosinhos stretch — pure city walking, nothing scenic. The walk itself is the start of the famous coastal boardwalks (passadiços) — flat wooden walkways right along the dunes, one of the most photogenic, easiest stretches on the whole Camino.
  • Tue 29 Sep Viana do Castelowalk · 16km · ~4h
    Taxi to Aguçadoura, walk to Marinhas via Apúlia, taxi on to Viana do Castelo.
    The walk passes Apúlia's palheiros — centuries-old wooden granaries built on stilts directly over the beach, arguably the single most photographed spot on this coast. Taxi trims a plain residential stretch beforehand, then covers the last ~13km into Viana, which is pleasant but similar to scenery already covered.
  • Wed 30 Sep A Guarda / Oia, Spainwalk · ~18km · ~4.5h
    Taxi Viana → Caminha, walk in from there.
    Taxi skips the full 26.8km Viana–Caminha stage — nice beach walking, but it repeats what the last two days already covered. The walk starts with a small ferry crossing the Rio Minho from Portugal into Spain, then follows wild, cliff-top Galician coastline past A Guarda to Oia's monastery, built right at the water's edge. Widely rated the single most scenic stage on the entire route.
  • Thu 1 Oct Vigowalk · 16km · ~4h
    Walk on to Baiona via the Cabo Silleiro lighthouse, taxi Baiona → Vigo.
    A dramatic lighthouse headland walk into Baiona, one of Galicia's prettiest fishing towns. Taxi covers the ~21km approach into Vigo itself, which is a working port city — worth arriving in, not worth walking into.
  • Fri 2 Oct Redondelawalk · 15.4km · ~4h
    Walk straight from Vigo — no taxi needed today.
    Quiet coastal and forest paths. This is also where the traditional "last 100km" pilgrim stretch begins — not that the certificate matters this time, but it's still the classic final approach to Santiago.
  • Sat 3 Oct Pontevedrawalk · 19.8km · ~5h
    Walk straight through — no taxi needed today.
    Widely considered one of the most beautiful stages on the whole Camino — woodland paths and the Ría de Pontevedra.
  • Sun 4 Oct Caldas de Reiswalk · ~17km · ~4.5h
    Taxi a few km out of Pontevedra, then walk in.
    Taxi skips a short, unremarkable urban exit from Pontevedra. Caldas de Reis itself is a classic pilgrim village, known for its thermal springs.
  • Mon 5 Oct Padrónwalk · 18.6km · ~4.5h
    Walk straight from Caldas de Reis — no taxi needed today.
    A historic town tied to the Camino's founding legend — where St. James's body is said to have arrived by boat.
  • Tue 6 Oct Santiago — finishwalk · ~16km · ~4h
    Taxi partway out of Padrón, walk the rest of the way in.
    Taxi skips ~8km of flat, unremarkable stretch out of Padrón. The walk in ends at the Cathedral — Pilgrim Mass, celebration dinner.
  • Wed 7 Oct Hometravel
    Fly Santiago → Lisbon → home, same day.
